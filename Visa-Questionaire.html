US Visa Questionnaire - Graduation Visit

Questions for Father

Visa Officer: Good morning. Why are you traveling to the United States?
Father: Good morning, Officer. We are traveling to attend my elder son's graduation ceremony.
Visa Officer: Who will be traveling with you?
Father: Myself, my wife, and my Son.
Visa Officer: What does your elder son do in the US?
Father: He is completing his Master's in Computer Science, and we are going to attend his graduation ceremony.
Visa Officer: What do you do in India?
Father: I work as a manager in a construction company in India, overseeing ongoing projects.
Visa Officer: Do you own a business?
Father: No, I am an employee. I have a stable managerial role and ongoing responsibilities.
Visa Officer: What does your wife do?
Father: She is a homemaker.
Visa Officer: What about your Son?
Father: He has completed his MBBS in India and is currently studying long-term coaching for a postgraduate medical program (PG) in India.
Visa Officer: How long do you plan to stay in the US?
Father: About 3 weeks, only for the graduation ceremony and some short sightseeing.
Visa Officer: Who will bear the expenses of your trip?
Father: I will bear all the expenses from my income and savings.
Visa Officer: Where will you stay in the US?
Father: We will be staying with my elder son at his accommodation in Atlanta [Georgia State].
Visa Officer: Do you have relatives in the US?
Father: Only my elder son, who is studying there.
Visa Officer: After attending the graduation, what will you do?
Father: We will return to India, as I have ongoing work projects here and my Son has to continue his studies.
Visa Officer: Why are you taking your Son along?
Father: It's a family event, and he also wants to attend his brother's graduation before returning to his studies.
Visa Officer: Have you traveled outside India before?
Father: [Answer truthfully – if yes, mention countries visited; if no, say "No, this will be our first international travel."]

Son: My focus is on PG in India. That's why I joined long-term coaching for a postgraduate medical program (PG) here. I am not planning to study abroad.

Father: No, Officer. My business and life are in India. We are only visiting for a short family occasion and will return.
10. VO (to whole family): What guarantee do we have that all of you will come back?

Golden Rules for Tricky Questions

  • Always show strong ties to India – work, studies, family, property.
  • Never show uncertainty – avoid "maybe" or "we'll see."
  • Keep answers consistent across family members.
  • Stay calm and smile – don't appear nervous.
